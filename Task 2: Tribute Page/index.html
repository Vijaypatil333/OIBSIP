Biography

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, popularly known as MS Dhoni, is a former Indian cricketer and captain of the Indian national team. He was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Bihar (now in Jharkhand), India. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest Indian cricketers of all time and is known for his calm demeanor and sharp cricketing brain.Dhoni made his debut for India in December 2004 in an ODI against Bangladesh. He was selected as the captain of the Indian cricket team in 2007 and led India to several significant victories, including the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cups, the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy. Dhoni is the only captain in the world to have won all three major ICC trophies.Dhoni is known for his exceptional wicket-keeping skills and is considered one of the best in the world. He is also known for his innovative captaincy and the ability to make bold decisions under pressure. Dhoni is also known for his finishing abilities, as he has won several matches for India with his batting in the final overs of a match.

Early Life

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, popularly known as MS Dhoni, was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, India. His father, Pan Singh, worked for MECON, while his mother, Devaki Devi, was a housewife. Dhoni has a sister and a brother.

Dhoni's love for cricket began at a young age, and he was drawn to the sport because of his school coach, Keshab Ranjan Banerjee, who recognized his talent and encouraged him to pursue the game. Dhoni attended DAV Jawahar Vidya Mandir, where he played for the school cricket team and later went on to play for the district and state teams. Despite his interest in cricket, Dhoni had to balance his passion for the sport with his studies. He graduated from St. Xavier's College in Ranchi with a degree in Commerce in 2003. Dhoni's early cricketing career was not without struggles. He faced financial difficulties, and at one point, worked as a ticket collector at Kharagpur railway station to support his cricketing dreams. However, his hard work and dedication paid off, and he was eventually selected to play for the Bihar cricket team in 1999.

Dhoni's cricketing talent was soon recognized, and he was selected to play for the Indian cricket team in 2004. His career took off after he was appointed captain of the Indian cricket team in 2007, and he went on to become one of the most successful and beloved cricketers in India's cricketing history. Dhoni's early life and struggles are a testament to his perseverance and his commitment to the sport of cricket. His passion for the game, coupled with his talent and hard work, paved the way for a remarkable career that continues to inspire millions of cricket fans around the world.

International Cricket

Mahendra Singh Dhoni is regarded as one of the greatest cricket captains India has ever produced. He made his international cricket debut in December 2004 against Bangladesh in an ODI match and went on to play 350 ODI matches, scoring 10,773 runs at an average of 50.57. He also played in 90 Test matches, scoring 4,876 runs at an average of 38.09, and in 98 T20 internationals, scoring 1,617 runs at an average of 37.60.

In August 2020, Dhoni announced his retirement from international cricket, bringing an end to a glorious career that spanned over 16 years. He retired after playing 350 ODIs, 90 Tests, and 98 T20Is for India. He scored over 10,000 runs in ODIs and more than 4,000 runs in Test cricket. Dhoni continues to play for the Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League (IPL), where he has won three IPL titles.

Cricket career

  • 1999

    Begining of Cricket journey

    Mahendra Singh Dhoni's cricket journey began in Ranchi, Jharkhand, where he grew up playing cricket with his schoolmates. It was his school coach, Keshab Ranjan Banerjee, who recognized Dhoni's talent and encouraged him to pursue the sport seriously. Dhoni went on to play for the school cricket team and later for the district and state teams. Mahendra Singh Dhoni's cricketing journey began in 1999 when he was selected to play for the Bihar cricket team. He quickly made a name for himself as a talented wicketkeeper-batsman and was soon picked up by the East Zone cricket team to play in the Duleep Trophy tournament.

    Despite his passion for cricket, Dhoni faced financial difficulties and had to balance his studies with his cricketing dreams. However, his dedication and hard work paid off, and he was eventually selected to play for the Bihar cricket team in 1999. This was just the beginning of what would be a remarkable cricketing career for Dhoni. Dhoni's cricketing talent soon caught the attention of selectors, and he was picked to play for the East Zone cricket team in the Duleep Trophy tournament. His performances in domestic cricket led to his selection in the Indian cricket team in 2004. Dhoni's rise to fame was meteoric, and he quickly established himself as a talented wicketkeeper-batsman, known for his aggressive batting style and his lightning-fast reflexes behind the stumps.

  • 2004-2005

    Selection in Indian Cricket Team

    In 2004, Dhoni was selected to play for the Indian cricket team in an ODI match against Bangladesh. He made an immediate impact, scoring 123 runs in his fifth ODI match against Pakistan in 2005, cementing his place in the Indian cricket team.

  • 2007

    Captain of Indian Cricket Team

    2007 was a pivotal year for Mahendra Singh Dhoni's cricket career as he was appointed as the captain of the Indian cricket team in all formats of the game. He took over the captaincy from Rahul Dravid and led India to a historic victory in the inaugural ICC World Twenty20 tournament in South Africa.Under Dhoni's captaincy, India played fearless cricket and defeated formidable teams like Pakistan, South Africa, Australia, and England to lift the trophy. Dhoni's calm and composed demeanor on the field was a standout feature, and he was credited for instilling a never-say-die attitude in the Indian team. Dhoni's success in the T20 format continued in the subsequent years as he led Chennai Super Kings to two IPL titles in 2010 and 2011. He also led India to the final of the 2014 ICC World Twenty20, where they lost to Sri Lanka.

  • 2010

    Leadership Skills

    In 2010, Mahendra Singh Dhoni continued to lead the Indian cricket team with distinction, and his leadership skills were on full display in a series of important matches throughout the year.Dhoni's leadership style was unconventional, and he introduced innovative tactics like using spinners in the powerplay overs, promoting lower-order batsmen up the order, and backing young players like Rohit Sharma and Suresh Raina. Dhoni's approach paid off, and India emerged as a dominant force in international cricket under his leadership.Apart from leading India to success in the T20 format, Dhoni also played an instrumental role in India's ODI success in 2010. India won a tri-series tournament in Sri Lanka that featured Sri Lanka and New Zealand. Dhoni's captaincy was once again crucial in the final against Sri Lanka, as India successfully chased down a target of 245 runs, with Dhoni scoring a crucial unbeaten 71*.

  • 2014

    ICC T20 Worldcup

    India hosted the 2014 ICC World Twenty20 tournament, and Dhoni's team came into the tournament as one of the favorites. However, they started the tournament on a disappointing note, losing their opening match to Pakistan. Despite this setback, Dhoni rallied his team and led from the front with some crucial contributions with both bat and gloves. India went on to win five consecutive matches, including a semifinal against South Africa, to set up a final clash against Sri Lanka. However, India were outclassed in the final by a superior Sri Lankan team, and Dhoni's valiant effort with the bat went in vain as India fell short by six wickets.

    In addition to his captaincy in international cricket, Dhoni also continued to lead Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League (IPL). In 2014, he led the team to the final for the sixth time in seven seasons, but they were defeated by Kolkata Knight Riders in a closely contested match.

    Dhoni retired from Test cricket in 2014, having played 90 Test matches and scored 4,876 runs.

  • 2018

    IPL title

    Dhoni continued to play for Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League (IPL). In the 2018 edition of the tournament, Dhoni led the team to their third IPL title, as they defeated Sunrisers Hyderabad in a thrilling final. Dhoni's leadership and contribution with both bat and gloves were crucial in the team's success, and he was named the Player of the Match in the final.

  • 2020

    Retired from International Cricket

    In August 2020, Dhoni announced his retirement from international cricket, bringing an end to a glorious career that spanned over 16 years. He retired after playing 350 ODIs, 90 Tests, and 98 T20Is for India. He scored over 10,000 runs in ODIs and more than 4,000 runs in Test cricket. Dhoni continues to play for the Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League (IPL), where he has won three IPL titles.
